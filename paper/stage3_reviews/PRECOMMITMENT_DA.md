# Phase 1 Precommitment - Devil's Advocate

## Contract Paraphrase

- D1 mandatory: search for design choices that make the advertised result inevitable or non-informative.
- D2 mandatory: test whether the domain contribution is merely sparse delta encoding under new terminology.
- D3 mandatory: construct the strongest alternative explanation and locate any break in the logic chain.
- D4 high: challenge whether a one-stack result supports field-level relevance.
- D5 normal: identify presentation choices that conceal assumptions or inflate significance.

## D1-D5 Scoring Plan

The statuses in the final report are contract risk flags, not a balanced recommendation or numerical paper score.

| dimension_id | what_to_look_for | what_triggers_block | what_triggers_warn |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1 | Tautological recovery, target-data leakage, post-hoc choices and missing falsification | Exact replay is guaranteed only because the certificate directly stores every needed answer and no nontrivial empirical claim remains | Exactness is tautological but sparsity is still a measurable contribution with narrow evidence |
| D2 | Delta encoding, replay logs, checkpointing and existing coupled-sampling alternatives | A direct existing method subsumes the contribution and is omitted | Novelty depends on packaging or evaluation rather than a distinct algorithmic principle |
| D3 | Hidden assumptions, circular construction and overgeneralization | The main conclusion does not follow even within the tested setting | Conclusions are valid only after stronger qualification |
| D4 | External validity, actual stakeholders, privacy and shared-state costs | The broad-impact case collapses when unstated deployment assumptions are made explicit | Relevance is plausible but not empirically demonstrated |
| D5 | Euphemistic naming, denominator shifts and buried limitations | Presentation materially misleads about what is transmitted or guaranteed | Critical assumptions are disclosed but not prominent enough |
