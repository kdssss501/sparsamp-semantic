# Phase 1 Precommitment - EIC

## Contract Paraphrase

- D1 mandatory: the study design, data provenance, statistical reporting and reproducibility package must support the stated inference.
- D2 mandatory: terminology, prior-work comparisons and domain claims must remain factually bounded.
- D3 mandatory: the central claim must follow from the results without circular or over-broad inference.
- D4 high: the work must explain why adjacent fields should care and substantiate any broad relevance.
- D5 normal: the paper must be organized, readable and suitable for the declared venue style.

## D1-D5 Scoring Plan

| dimension_id | what_to_look_for | what_triggers_block | what_triggers_warn |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1 | Evidence scale, independent validation, reproducible protocol and estimand clarity | The central empirical result is not reproducible or the design cannot test the stated claim | Internally valid evidence is too narrow for the venue-level claim |
| D2 | Novelty against replay logs, delta encoding, nondeterminism mitigation and verification | The claimed contribution is already established or domain terminology is materially wrong | Novelty is plausible but comparisons or literature coverage are incomplete |
| D3 | Alignment among title, abstract, results, limitations and conclusion | Main conclusion exceeds or contradicts the evidence | Core claim is sound but significance language needs narrowing |
| D4 | Broad significance beyond one model/GPU stack | A high-selectivity multidisciplinary framing has no substantiated audience beyond the local artifact | Adjacent-field relevance is plausible but external evidence is missing |
| D5 | Manuscript structure, figures, author metadata and submission readiness | Presentation prevents assessment of the contribution | Placeholders, dense exposition or venue mismatch remain |
